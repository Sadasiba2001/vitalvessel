Blood Donation

Blood banks collect and store donated blood from voluntary donors. They organize blood donation drives and accept walk-in donors to ensure a steady supply of blood for medical emergencies, surgeries, and patients with various medical conditions.

Blood Typing and Crossmatching

Blood banks perform blood typing and crossmatching to determine a patient's blood type and compatibility with available blood products. This is crucial to ensure safe transfusions.

Emergency Response

Blood banks play a vital role in emergency response situations, providing blood products quickly to hospitals in cases of accidents, natural disasters, or other emergencies.

Antibody Screening

Blood banks screen donated blood for antibodies and infectious diseases, ensuring that only safe blood products are distributed for transfusion.

Platelet Apheresis

Blood banks may offer platelet apheresis, a procedure where platelets are collected from a donor and the remaining blood components are returned to the donor. This is often used for patients with certain medical conditions.

Donor Care and Support

Blood banks educate and support blood donors, ensuring they have a positive experience and feel valued for their contributions. They may also provide refreshments and post-donation care.
